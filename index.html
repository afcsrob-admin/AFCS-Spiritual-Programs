Who We Are

The School as a Daily Church

At AFCS, we believe spiritual growth is not just an event but a lifelong journey. Every devotion, Chapel Hour, small group, and outreach is not for tradition's sake — but for worship.

Our vision is to help every Faithian know Jesus, grow in intimacy with Him, and influence others through servant leadership. We believe the school is a sacred space where faith and learning come together.

Direction & Purpose

Vision & Mission

👁️

Vision

AFCS is committed to prepare and nurture students to be torchbearers of the Gospel of Jesus Christ who will shed light to their communities and throughout the world through various leadership opportunities and ministries according to God's sovereign purpose and will.

🎯

Mission

To provide Christian education for students to be biblically founded, academically prepared, globally competent, and service-oriented.

🏁

Goal

For Faithians to be authentic Torchbearers of the Gospel of Jesus Christ by ensuring a clear understanding and a purposeful living out of the Gospel.

📖

Objectives

  • 1

    Clearly present and help students fully understand the Gospel through the Eight Essential Truths.

  • 2

    Follow a progressive spiritual development guide for the fulfilment of God's plan for each Faithian.

  • 3

    Train students to do missionary work — preach the gospel, initiate small groups, and mentor the next generation.

Core Framework

Key Areas of Spiritual Formation

From Prep to Grade 10, every Faithian is guided, challenged, and nurtured in these four essential spiritual standards — rooted in Matthew 5:16.

📚
Goal: Be Aware of His Story

1. Spiritual Understanding

Faithians grow in a clear, age-appropriate understanding of the Gospel centered on the Eight Essential Truths: God, Man, Sin, Death, Christ, Cross, Faith, and Life.

"Let the message of Christ dwell among you richly..." — Colossians 3:16
🙌
Goal: Be Purposeful & Competent

2. Spiritual Participation

Faithians regularly participate in prayer, worship, devotions, community service, and small group interaction — practicing their faith within school and beyond.

"Do not give up meeting together..." — Hebrews 10:25
📈
Goal: Be Principled & Christ-like

3. Spiritual Progress

Growth is measured through the Evangelism & Discipleship Scale — an intentional guide from Gospel understanding to Gospel living, from knowing Christ to sharing Christ.

"...entrust to faithful men, who will be able to teach others also." — 2 Timothy 2:2
🕊️
Goal: Be Good Stewards & Torchbearers

4. Spiritual Discipline

Faithians develop lifelong habits: prayer, Scripture reading, meditation, confession, and fasting — staying spiritually rooted as they grow in independence.

"Discipline yourself for the purpose of godliness." — 1 Timothy 4:7

Effective S.Y. 2026 onwards, every Christian Education lesson is structured around the 3Cs Teaching Method — a Gospel-rooted framework that moves students from divine purpose, through biblical conviction, to real-world commission.

"Go therefore and make disciples of all nations..." — Matthew 28:19 · The Great Commission is not just a program — it is the pattern for every lesson.

📣
STAGE 1

C-alling

The Philosophy

Every lesson begins with a divine call. Before content is delivered, students are invited to see themselves within God's larger story — understanding what God is calling them to know, become, or embody through this truth.

Rooted in Scripture and AFCS's Torchbearer identity, C-alling answers the essential "why": Why does this lesson matter for eternity? Why is this essential for a Faithian's purpose and mission?

TEACHER'S ROLE

Cast the vision. Ground the lesson in God's call. Connect truth to the student's God-given identity and purpose as a Torchbearer.

🔥
STAGE 2

C-onviction

The Core

This is where truth does its transforming work. Through guided instruction, Scripture engagement, discussion, and reflection, students are led from head knowledge to heart conviction.

C-onviction includes the necessary learning steps: exploring the content, wrestling with its implications, evaluating it against Scripture, and arriving at a personal biblical stance. This is where the lesson moves from information to inner transformation.

TEACHER'S ROLE

Guide students through the content. Facilitate discovery. Prompt higher-order thinking. Ensure truth lands in both mind and heart.

🌍
STAGE 3

C-ommission

The Sending

Every lesson ends with a sending. Modeled after the Great Commission (Matt. 28:19-20), C-ommission equips Faithians to take what they've learned out of the classroom and into their world.

Students apply truth in their relationships, decisions, acts of service, and Gospel witness. C-ommission turns conviction into action, making every student a walking embodiment of the lesson — a little missionary wherever they go.

TEACHER'S ROLE

Send students out equipped. Bridge truth to life. Inspire specific action — in family, peer group, outreach, or daily decisions.
